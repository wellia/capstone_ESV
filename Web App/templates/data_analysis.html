SpanLength

Span length represents the distance between two electrical poles. This graph shows the span length distribution in non-compliant cases. As seen, the amount of non-compliant cases piled up in the areas where the spans were low. More and more non-compliant cases subsided as the span length expended. The peak of non-compliant cases extended between 30 to 50.

We can infer that span length had a noticeable pattern that can be used in tracking non-compliant cases.

ClearanceSpace

This graph illustrates Clearance Space distribution in non-compliant cases. Evidentially the amount of non-compliant cases was highest at the range between 0-180. As the clearance space increased, the non-compliant cases began to drop and almost reached to zero when the range was significantly higher i.e. from 1080-1800.

Therefore, this distribution gives us a noticeable pattern of clearance space in relation to non-compliant cases and we can infer that low clearance space is very likely to be responsible for the non-compliant cases.

MaintainedBy

This pie chart shows the proportional percentage of non-complaint cases, either maintained by council, company, or both. Within the area which was maintained by both council and company, the percentage of non-complaint case was significantly low i.e. 3%. The percentage became higher when the areas were maintained by company or council individually.

Therefore, it gives a clear sign that joined collaboration between both parties significantly contributes to reduction of non-compliant cases.

ResponsibleCouncil

This plot compares the percentage of non-complaint cases by responsible councils for two different programs i.e. LBRAs and HBRAs. The cases with HBRAs were 100% compliant by responsible councils. However, within LBRAs program, there were 15 councils who were found to be non-compliant. Boroondara was on the lead with up to 18% of non-compliant cases as compared to others.

Overall, non-compliant cases tended to happen only in LBRA program type, and a number of councils were found to contribute to this scenario.

ResponsibleCouncil

This graph represents the councils responsible for both high risk (HRNC) and low risk (LR) of non-complaint cases combined. Red color shows HRNC while white shows NC count. Most of council tends to have a balance of HRNC and NC, except that of Boroondara whose high-risk cases outweighed the low-risk ones.

In summary, this graph reveals the critical information that spotlights the high and low risk of non-compliant cases among responsible councils and helps in prioritizing plan for next inspection.

SpanVoltages

This chart demonstrates the amount of non-compliant cases within each type of span voltages which further classified into high risk (HRNC) and low risk (NC) scenario. As seen in the graph, low voltage [LV] locations shared the largest proportion of non-compliance, both in HRNC and NC cases. High voltage [HV] locations were responsible for the second largest proportion in NC cases, while locations that ran [HV, LV] similarly claimed the second rank in HRNC cases.

The combination of 20 other types of locations (Others) did not seem to have any signification contribution to these non-compliant cases. In the order of HRNC we notice that [LV] locations need the greatest attention and initiatives, followed by [HV, LV], then [HV] locations, and later other type of locations.

Key assumptions in relation to the nature of vegetation and incidents

1. If no inspections are done in last 3 financial years, it is most likely that incident will occur at the locations where vegetation exists near the electrical line.
2. Places that have HIGH incident rate related to vegetation-contact but have LOW inspection rate are strongly recommended for frequent inspection.
3. If there have been many non-compliant inspections in the last 3 years, it tends to stay non-compliant in the following year.
4. If the several incidents have occurred in last 3 financial years, it is most likely to happen again in the following year.
5. The rate of non-compliance for lines that have undergone hazard assessment is between 16% to 17%. However, non-compliance of lines without hazard assessment could NOT be done because of CLT.
6. Between single and multiple trees, non-compliant lines with multiple trees are 3% to 7% more than that with single tree.
7. Network provider with higher incident rates requires higher priority of inspection.
